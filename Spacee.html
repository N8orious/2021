Project Brief

As engaging with colleagues, friends and family members are mediated through conversational spaces, the static rectangular grids and conversational branches of existing interfaces are being challenged.

As makers of applications, platforms and experiences in spaces, this project expects you to engage with the old and new ecosystem of conversational-collaborative computer mediated spaces. While the makers of these spaces come with their own principles and philosophies, there is a dearth of spaces that explore away from the conventional static spaces

The space has to engage with fluid modes of collaboration and conversation while we engage with each other for work. But should also demonstrate its use for hanging out, facilitating play, humour and catching up with each other remotely.

What I intend to Build?

A better way to collaborate, A better way to engage and work together. A way that unhooked people from the traditional conversational branches. I want to build a space that deals with both synchronous and asynchronous engagements.

Built for both a professional environment and a fun atmosphere without compromising on the experience or the interface.
Why?

Facilitating remote working or hanging out through traditional conversational branches were challenged during the COVID19 situation in 2020. People were locked up in their houses and were not able to go to professional workspaces or meet others. Everything had to be shifted online and suddenly remote working was a big challenge that was encountered by every working professional. And people still wanted to meet and hangout with their friends and family.

However, People continued to use existing conversational applications and multiple tools to support their remote working. And there was not a single application which helped professionals See, talk to, and collaborate with their team all at one single spot. Hence, I decided to to build a better way to collaborate, engage and work together all in a single application.

Not only did I come up with a better way, I designed the entire brand behind the product, and make sure the brand is reflected throughout the product. I also worked on the Business model of the product and shaped the Interface and Experience to cater it perfectly.

Lets get started!

The first thing I want to talk about is my process, which is specifically tailored to this project. I personally don't believe in having a universal UX design process which will apply to all projects.

As I was creating an application which will facilitate remote working, I wanted it to not look very different from what professional applications look like right now, So that I can reduce the learning curve involved.

The Approach

Donâ€™t reinvent the UX wheel: look at what your competitors are doing right

When you're starting out from scratch with a new product idea, you want the familiarity factor to be on your side. When your potential customers try out your product, If they get stuck because you are making them go through a completely new experience, you are preventing them from seeing the potential value that your solution could bring them

Looking at existing interfaces that users are comfortable with and trying to build over it with familiar interactions, affordances, and building towards a very simple and intuitive interface

The Process

I sat down and created the entire roadmap of this project in the given timeline of four weeks. I created a detailed plan with various tasks, techniques and key factors to make sure flawless results for my product.

Rabbit Holing

Rabbit holing is a term I got from one of my mentors. I use it to describe the research phase of the project. This will include all the digging around, analyzing where your product will exactly fit in.

Action Items:
  1. Benchmarking existing tools
  2. Classifying Affordances and correlating it with needs that are not mapped
  3. Ideating possible scenarios and contextualize the platform using storyboards
